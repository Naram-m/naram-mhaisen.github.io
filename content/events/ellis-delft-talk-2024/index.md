abstract: |
  This talk addresses one of the most general frameworks for modeling sequential
  decision problems: online learning via (non-)convex optimization. We focus on
  a recent and practical twist on online learning — *optimism* — which centers
  on incorporating untrusted machine-learning advice into the learning process.
  The goal is simple yet ambitious: boost performance when the advice proves
  accurate, while preserving a worst-case guarantee should it fail. We present
  recent results from this line of work, including optimistic learning
  algorithms for continuous, discrete, and stateful environments, alongside
  practical case studies in network optimization and control.
